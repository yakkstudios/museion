The Six Scoring Dimensions

The Kaize Cabal Risk Index is built from six independently observable signal types, each weighted by how directly they correlate with documented retail harm. Total weight sums to 100%.

28% Weight
01 — Rug Association Frequency
The most important dimension — the most direct measure of harm already done. If a KOL promoted 12 tokens and 7 later appeared in the rug ledger, that is a pattern. Tokens are classified as rugs using structural criteria only: LP removed, dev wallet dumped, mint authority not renounced. Price decline alone is not sufficient. Binary, deterministic, auditable.
25% Weight
02 — Exit Asymmetry
Did the person promoting the token profit while their followers lost? Three signals tracked: entry timing (did the wallet buy before the promo post?), exit timing (did they sell near ATH while the post was still getting engagement?), and follower loss correlation (statistical relationship between their exit and community wallet drawdown). Extraction scoring from observable wallet behaviour.
18% Weight
03 — Structural Exploitation
Not all promotions carry equal harm potential. This dimension scores the mechanism being promoted, not just the outcome. Pump.fun bonding curves, "fair launches" where dev wallets loaded pre-announcement, coordinated multi-KOL launches timed within minutes, and repeated use of identical shill templates across different tokens — all scored. Pattern recognition, not outcome analysis.
15% Weight
04 — Disclosure Honesty
Under UK FCA rules, any financial promotion — including social media posts promoting cryptoassets to UK audiences — requires clear, fair, and not misleading disclosure. "DYOR NFA" is not a disclosure. It is a shield. This dimension measures the ratio of paid posts to disclosed posts. The ratio is the score. Binary and auditable.
7% Weight
05 — Network Clustering
Graph analysis of wallet transaction history. Specifically: does this wallet repeatedly transact with addresses in YAKK's Cabal Wallet Registry? A single shared transaction with a flagged wallet is an info signal. Multiple co-transactions across multiple launches, combined with high scores on other dimensions, becomes evidence of embedded network membership. Not guilt by association — structural proximity scoring.
7% Weight
06 — Regulatory Proximity
KOLs operating from lighter-regulation jurisdictions while deliberately targeting UK or EU audiences with non-compliant promotions are engaged in regulatory arbitrage. Weighted lower because harder to evidence than on-chain behaviour — but material when combined with high scores in other dimensions. Adds contextual weight, not primary scoring.

The Investigation Pipeline

Every cabal investigation follows a structured pipeline. The six specialist models of the Original Six council (Kaize, Morra, Zephyr, Riven, Sable, Mnemos) each own a stage of the process.

01
Signal Ingestion — Zephyr
Real-time social and on-chain monitoring. GeckoTerminal pool data cross-referenced with live X cashtag sentiment. Influencer coordination window mapping (≥5 Tier-2 influencers, same hashtag, within 15-minute window). Hard 1500ms timeout — pipeline continues without social context if exceeded.
02
FeatureVector Extraction — Sable
Dual-mode engine. Mode A extracts 8-dimensional on-chain signals from Helius RPC: gini_coefficient holder_count sniper_density lp_lock_pct dev_wallet_pct tx_velocity mint_authority freeze_authority. Mode B maps to deterministic risk bands. Strips benign heavy-hitters (CEX wallets, known market makers) before scoring.
03
Historical Analog Retrieval — Mnemos
Alexandria Archive search for historical patterns matching the current FeatureVector signature. Top 3 analog matches returned with extraction amounts, pattern type, and outcome. Repeat wallet address detection. MEMMA freshness loop verifies record validity before retrieval.
04
Synthesis & Scoring — Kaize
Sovereign orchestrator. Ingests all specialist outputs, applies Hybrid Symmetry Scoring across the six dimensions, runs the MEMMA verification loop, and issues the final Cabal Risk Index score with confidence rating and evidence summary. 40% of vote weight. Issues final Intelligence Report.
05
Adversarial Red-Team — Riven
Staged review. Stage 1: silent monitoring of the council output, anomaly accumulation. Stage 2: full adversarial review — volume spoofing audit, Jaccard trap check, Nash-equilibrium attack search. Holds veto power over Kaize's final score. If Riven vetoes, Morra escalates.
06
Escalation — Morra
Dormant by default. Activates on council deadlock or when Riven vetoes the primary score. Multi-hop graph traversal for complex obfuscation mapping. Maintains Intrinsic Memory isolated from Kaize's conversation history. Also serves as primary artifact builder for published investigation reports.

Museion On-Chain Architecture

Alongside the KOL/social scoring system above, Museion runs a separate on-chain detection layer — a continuous background intelligence pipeline that pulls live signals from Helius RPC, normalises them through Sable's FeatureVector extractor, and compounds them across five independent on-chain detectors. Borrowed from Agent-Z's open-source architecture: compound independent evidence streams rather than relying on a single opaque score. An alert fires only when two or more detectors independently corroborate the same anomaly pattern.

Helius MCP Server
TypeScript + Python dual-impl. Paginated getTransactionsForAddress, webhook subscriptions, raw-to-FeatureVector extraction. Exponential backoff + jitter on all RPC calls.
Sable FeatureVector
Shannon entropy, stable wallet fingerprinting, volume envelope normalisation, trade-timing clustering. Deterministic output schema — same input always produces same vector.
Detector Stack ×5
Pre-unlock LP rotation, OTC-shadow exits, synchronised trade fingerprinting, TWAP-masking detector, governance power concentration. Each operates independently and produces a binary flag + confidence score.
Cabal Scorer
Composite risk score using compound OR logic — triggers only when 2+ detectors fire simultaneously. Outputs score + evidence chain + confidence band (LOW / MED / HIGH / CRITICAL).
Adversarial Review
Kaize + Justitia council review. False-positive vs false-negative tracks separated. Human sign-off required before any public advisory is issued.
HUD Surface
Cabal Starburst Visualiser (SSE-streamed), VAR readout, Sniper Density map. Real-time via Helius webhook → Server-Sent Events → React client.

The Five On-Chain Heuristics

Each detector operates independently and produces a binary flag plus a confidence score. The Cabal Scorer compounds them — a single detector never triggers a public alert alone. Validated precision benchmarks from Flashbots research at F1=0.943 at 0.5% threshold, 3600s synchronisation window.

H-01
Pre-Unlock LP Rotation
Monitors insiders reducing LP, delegating tokens, or unstaking within a 72-hour window before a scheduled unlock event. Validated as the highest-precision single detector for exit orchestration. Watches for lp_delta_72h deviating below threshold relative to position size.
CRITICAL
H-02
OTC-Shadow Exit
Detects sudden wallet-to-wallet flows between common-funded addresses that bypass DEX and CEX channels entirely. Signature of coordinated insider cash-out before public awareness. Tracked via otc_flow_usd field — spikes above baseline trigger the flag.
HIGH
H-03
Synchronised Trade Fingerprinting
Identifies 2+ addresses executing near-identical trades within a 3-second window. sync_score measures 0–1 correlation. Flashbots-validated precision F1=0.943 at 0.5% threshold, 3600s window. Deterministic — no inference required once the timing pattern fires.
HIGH
H-04
TWAP-Masking Detector
Volume envelope analysis to detect fragmented insider exits that stay individually below DEX alert thresholds but cumulatively constitute a coordinated exit. twap_mask boolean flags patterns where individual trades look organic but the cumulative envelope does not.
MEDIUM
H-05
Governance Power Concentration
Tracks voting power centralisation preceding protocol parameter changes or "pivot" proposals. gov_power_delta measures weight change. Frequently precedes retroactive treasury redirections dressed as governance votes.
MEDIUM

FeatureVector Output Schema

Sable produces a normalised FeatureVector for every wallet analysed — the deterministic intermediate format that both the KOL scoring system and the Museion on-chain detector stack consume. Same input, same output, every time.

The Asymmetric Tolerance Principle

The detection system operates with a deliberate asymmetry: it errs toward flagging rather than silence. Reducing sensitivity to eliminate false positives will silently increase false negatives — and a missed cabal has vastly higher human cost than a false alert reviewed and cleared by the adversarial layer. Calibration should improve the nullifier list and adversarial prompts independently, never by globally lowering sensitivity thresholds.

Determinism Rule — Locked In

YAKK's forensics engine operates on a strict determinism-only rule. Gini coefficient above 0.85 = High Risk. Fewer than 5 holders = High Risk. These thresholds are locked in the codebase and cannot be overridden by probabilistic logic, AI inference, or editorial judgment. The score is the score because the data is the data. This is the rule that makes the system legally defensible and practically trustworthy.
