The Empire HUD
Scroll 019 · Interface & Embeds · YAKK Studios · April 2026
The surface of the empire is a HUD, not a feed. Voice-first, telemetry-rich, and built to be embedded inside partner products — a pane of glass where a person sees their position, their risk, and their next decision without being sold anything.

Why a HUD, Not a Feed

A feed is built for dwell time. A HUD is built for decisions. Feeds reward scrolling, recommendation loops, and emotional hooks — the same attention economics that funnel retail into coordinated exits. A HUD reverses the polarity: the surface exists to compress state into something a person can read in two seconds and act on in ten.

The Æther HUD collapses five layers — wallet state, market microstructure, holder distribution, narrative signal, and council verdict — into a single interface that a holder can glance at before they commit capital, and a partner can embed without breaking their own product's voice.

Four Surface Laws

Voice-First
Every HUD state must be speakable in one clean sentence by Aether. If it can't be spoken, it doesn't belong on the surface. This forces clarity at design time and keeps the interface legible to the subset of users who prefer audio over pixels.
Telemetry Over Tiles
The HUD shows state, not posts. Prices, Gini, LP lock, cluster convergence, verdict — not tweets, not engagement, not "trending" noise. Telemetry is the evidence. Posts are the thing it protects you from.
Embeddable by Default
The HUD ships as a signed embed — StreamLock can drop it next to a creator's token, StakePoint can drop it next to a validator. The brand-frame adapts; the evidence layer doesn't. The embed is cryptographically attested back to the council that generated it.
Refusable
Any surface the HUD presents can be collapsed or silenced. Aether never pins itself to the top of a user's attention. A HUD that can't be dismissed becomes a feed. A HUD that can be dismissed and returned to stays trustworthy.
"A feed is paid for by your attention. A HUD is paid for by your clarity. Choose carefully what stares back at you all day."

Partner Surface Spec

The HUD is built as a set of signed panels that partners embed without giving up their brand. Each panel carries its own evidence pointer, its own council-attested verdict, and its own "ask-Æther" action. Drop it into a chat surface, a validator page, a creator's token page, or a merchant checkout — and the same integrity contract holds everywhere.

HUD Signal Hierarchy

Not every signal deserves the same surface. Aether's HUD sorts state into three tiers — ambient (always visible, low-cost), active (surfaced when a threshold is crossed), and urgent (voice-delivered, un-ignorable). Most of the time the HUD is calm. When it is not calm, it is because the evidence is loud — and loudness is earned, never bought.

Ambient · Always
Price, Gini, LP status, council verdict, last-updated timestamp. Five telemetry fields at a glance. No motion, no colour flash. The ground state of the HUD is rest.
Active · On Threshold
A cluster forms. A pool migrates. A KOL pre-load hits. The ambient field that represents it gains a single colour change and a short Aether sentence. Still dismissable. Still refusable.
Urgent · Voice + Haptic
An exit is in progress. The LP unlock window opens. A wallet the council watches wakes. Aether speaks, unsolicited, in one sentence: what happened, what to do. The user can silence — once — and the evidence is archived regardless.
Precedent · AS-063 Courier Network. The Roman cursus publicus worked because a message carried the same weight at the edge of the empire as at its centre. The HUD is the cursus publicus for on-chain truth: whatever verdict the council returns at the core of Æther is the same verdict that reaches the user on a partner surface a thousand miles away. No quality loss. No selective silence. Same evidence, same voice, everywhere it lands.
Partner Hook. For StreamLock, the HUD is how a viewer sees through a creator's token pitch in the same frame as the creator is pitching — trust is compounded, not leaked. For StakePoint, the HUD is how a staker sees validator quality as a weather report, not a marketing page. Both embeds ship with council attestation, so what a partner shows their user is the same thing Aether would say if asked directly.

What the HUD Refuses

As much as the HUD is defined by what it shows, it is defined by what it will not. It will not show engagement metrics as a quality signal. It will not show "trending" as a safety signal. It will not push notifications for dwell-time reasons. It will not let a partner override a red verdict with a green badge. The interface carries the same covenant as the council: non-greedy, evidence-bound, refusable.

"A HUD that lies faster than a feed is worse than a feed. Surfaces inherit the morality of what generates them."
